# Figure Accessibility Descriptions

This document provides detailed alternative text descriptions for all images used in the ACCESS KNOB project and manuscript, following the **ACM SIGACCESS Guidance on Describing Figures**.

---

## 1. ProfileShapes.png
* **Context**: Used to illustrate the customizer's support for tactile shape-coding.
* **Description**: A vector diagram showing eight different cross-sectional geometric profiles for the customizer body. From left to right, the profiles are:
  - **Triangle**: A three-sided polygon with sharp corners.
  - **Diamond/Square**: A four-sided diamond shape.
  - **Pentagon**: A regular five-sided polygon.
  - **Hexagon**: A regular six-sided polygon.
  - **Octagon**: A regular eight-sided polygon.
  - **Star**: A symmetric star profile with alternating pointed extensions.
  - **Gear/Fluted**: A circular shape with gear-like teeth protruding outwards.
  - **Circle**: A standard smooth circular profile.
  Each shape is rendered in a solid dark blue fill with a bright cyan outer boundary outline, highlighting the physical profile.

## 2. OutputDetailsScreen.png
* **Context**: Shows the browser interface, mutation grid, and detail configuration sidebar.
* **Description**: A screenshot of the web-based ACCESS KNOB customization application. The interface has two main panels on a dark background:
  - **Left/Main Panel**: A grid displaying 12 different variations of 3D-rendered knobs generated by the mutation engine. Each variation has a different combination of shape, diameter, and groove settings, numbered from K793-001 to K793-012. 
  - **Right Panel (Inspector)**: A configuration sidebar showing details for the selected knob variant (K793-001). It displays a high-fidelity 3D viewport at the top showing a blue transparent blueprint model with glowing cyan outline highlights. Below the viewport are slider controls and numerical inputs for adjusting dimensions, including Outer Diameter (35mm), Height (22mm), Shape (Hexagon), and Shaft Bore profile (6mm D-shaft).

---

## 3. SwapInSlideOver.png
* **Context**: Explains the difference between Swap-In and Slide-Over mounting strategies.
* **Description**: A side-by-side comparative technical diagram illustrating the two mounting methods:
  - **Swap-In Mode (Left)**: Shows a 3D model of a custom knob body with a hollow central cavity specifically sized to press-fit directly onto the bare metal potentiometer shaft of a synthesizer. The shaft stands alone, and the knob slides down to cover it.
  - **Slide-Over Mode (Right)**: Shows a 3D model of an adaptive sleeve designed to fit over the factory-installed potentiometer knob without removing it. The interior cavity of the sleeve matches the outer profile and grip patterns of the original knob, allowing the musician to slip the customizer on top without tool disassembly.

---

## 4. PalmDragging.png
* **Context**: Demonstrates ergonomic palm-dragging interaction enabled by the large knob diameter.
* **Description**: A close-up photograph of a musician's hand interacting with a custom 3D-printed knob installed on a modular synthesizer panel. The knob is large (approximately 45mm in diameter) with deep, star-patterned flutes around its perimeter. The musician is using the flat palm of their hand pressed against the top and sides of the knob to rotate it, demonstrating how the increased diameter and grip depth allow control actuation without requiring a fine-motor pinch or finger-grip strength.

---

## 5. SmallKnobsSecuredWithM3Screws.png
* **Context**: Shows high-density knobs and mechanical set-screw mounts.
* **Description**: A photograph of a dense array of small customizer knobs mounted on a synthesizer module. The knobs are about 18mm in diameter, colored in bright orange, and have a smooth surface finish. On the side of each knob, a small threaded hole holds a metal M3 set screw, which is tightened against the potentiometer shaft. This secures the knob firmly in place and prevents slipping under rotation force.

---

## 6. LaserCutOuts.png
* **Context**: Explains the layered vector export system.
* **Description**: A photo of several laser-cut wooden rings and alignment rods laid out on a table. The pieces represent slices of a custom knob exported as a 2D SVG vector stack:
  - **Layer Rings**: A series of circular wooden rings of varying outer diameters, each with a central circular hole (the shaft bore) and two small square alignment slots punched on opposite sides of the central hole.
  - **Alignment Rods**: Two thin, rectangular wooden pegs whose width matches the slots in the rings. When the rings are stacked vertically, these rods insert through the alignment slots to keep all layers perfectly centered and locked rotationally while gluing.

## 7. MaterialTable.png
* **Context**: Evaluates physical material properties and tactile feedback for 3D-printed and laser-cut controls.
* **Description**: A table with a pink header row comparing various materials across five columns: **Material**, **Process**, **Rigidity**, **Friction**, and **Tactile Feedback & Damping**. The rows contain:
  - **PLA** (3D Print): Medium rigidity, medium friction, hard contact, crisp edges, and poor damping.
  - **PETG** (3D Print): High rigidity, medium friction, tough structure, hard contact, and poor damping.
  - **TPU** (3D Print): Low rigidity, high friction, soft elastomeric grip, and high damping.
  - **Basswood** (Laser-Cut): Medium rigidity, medium-high friction, warm organic feel, and good damping.
  - **Acrylic** (Laser-Cut): High rigidity, low friction, hard polished surface, and poor damping.
  - **Foam** (Laser-Cut): Very low rigidity, low friction, spongy texture, and excellent vibration absorption.
  - **Cardboard** (Laser-Cut): Low rigidity, medium-high friction, matte texture, high damping, and low durability.
  - **Cork** (Laser-Cut): Low-medium rigidity, high friction, textured grip, and excellent damping and shock absorption.
