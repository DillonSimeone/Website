The revelation of his genesis should, by all biological and psychological metrics, have shattered Sephiroth’s mind.

Sitting in the subterranean gloom of the Shinra Mansion, surrounded by the crumbling, physical data files of the Jenova Project, the First Class SOLDIER processed the reality of his existence. He was not human. He was an amalgamation, built around the cellular framework of an extraterrestrial entity. And that entity’s ultimate directive, as outlined in Gast’s sprawling hypotheses, was to consume the planet’s lifeforce and utilize the husk as a celestial vehicle.

Sephiroth set the report down on the dusty mahogany desk. He steepled his gloved fingers.

A vehicle, he thought.

The concept was fundamentally absurd. The logistical overhead of piloting a planetary mass through the vacuum of space defied basic propulsion mechanics. If the entity consumed all available terrestrial energy simply to achieve escape velocity or establish a sustainable trajectory, there would be zero surplus for navigational thrust, let alone auxiliary life support for the organism itself. The system architecture was inherently flawed. What, exactly, would one do with a dead, drifting rock once in the void?

To visualize the energy transfer required for such a colossal misuse of resources, Sephiroth reached into his tactical harness. Because of his rank and the sheer breadth of his deployments, Sephiroth carried a localized arsenal of crystallized Mako that far exceeded the requisition limits of any standard researcher or infantryman. He possessed dozens of orbs: command, magic, independent, and support.

He began to arrange them on the desk, treating the glowing spheres as nodes in a physical circuit diagram.

He placed an 'Elemental' paired with 'Lightning' to represent the primary ignition sequence, routing the theoretical throughput into an 'HP Absorb' to simulate the parasitic draw Jenova would require. His vast field experience guided his hands instinctively. He knew the precise harmonic frequencies of these stones. He knew how they interacted under stress. When he noticed a potential cascade failure in his mock setup—a dead-end route that would act as a magical short to ground—he instinctively bridged the gap with a 'Time' materia to regulate the flow, closing the circuit with a cluster of 'Magic Plus' nodes.

He applied a micro-fraction of his own internal Mako resonance to initialize the array, expecting the stones to light up briefly and then dissipate, demonstrating the swift entropic decay of Jenova's grand design.

The orbs flared. They cycled.

Then, the glow intensified.

Sephiroth frowned. He withdrew his hand, cutting off the supply of his own energy. The system should have immediately powered down. Instead, the high-pitched hum of active magic deepened into a resonant, physical vibration that rattled the inkwells on the desk.

He analyzed the output. The 'Lightning' node was feeding into the 'Absorb', which was multiplying through the support stones, being temporally dilated by the 'Time' node, and feeding back into the 'Lightning' node.

The localized temperature in the room was rising. The light was blinding. Sephiroth shielded his eyes, his mind rapidly calculating the variables.

Input: Zero.
Output: Exponentially increasing.

"Overunity," Sephiroth murmured to the empty room. "A positive return."

It was a violation of the most fundamental laws of thermodynamics. It was a perpetual motion machine, constructed on a dusty desk in a forgotten basement, purely by accident.

The philosophical weight of his alien mother vanished instantly from his mind. Jenova was a biological curiosity, yes, but this... this was a paradigm shift that rewrote the physics of the universe. Sephiroth pulled up a chair. He began to take notes.

Days bled into weeks. The Shinra Electric Power Company, receiving no scheduled communications from Nibelheim, classified their greatest asset as Missing In Action. The Board assumed the worst: defection, insanity, or death.

When a specialized squad of Shinra infantry finally breached the doors of the mansion and descended into the basement, weapons drawn and trembling, they did not find a rogue super-soldier plotting the destruction of the world.

They found Sephiroth wearing a pair of reading glasses, surrounded by hundreds of pages of mathematical proofs, carefully measuring the ambient voltage of a glowing, self-sustaining ring of rocks with a specialized multimeter.

"Sir?" the squad leader stammered, lowering his rifle.

"Do not disrupt the ambient airflow," Sephiroth said without looking up. "The thermal regulation is incredibly delicate."

It took another three days for Professor Hojo to be flown out from Midgar. The head of the Science Department burst into the basement, his lab coat billowing, expecting to find the culmination of his twisted genetic legacy.

Instead, Hojo stopped dead in his tracks. The basement hummed with enough clean, infinite energy to power the entirety of Midgar's Sector 8.

Hojo pushed his glasses up his nose. He stared at the arrangement of materia. He walked around the desk. He looked under it. He looked for hidden cables, for a hidden Mako reactor feed, for any rational explanation. He stared at the readouts on the monitoring equipment Sephiroth had requisitioned from the local reactor.

"This is impossible," Hojo whispered, his voice trembling with a mixture of awe and scientific outrage. "The conservation of energy... you cannot generate output without an equivalent or greater input. The system is generating more energy than it is using. It is impossible."

Sephiroth, leaning back in the creaking wooden chair, tapped his pen against his clipboard. He looked at the infinite energy loop, and then looked at his biological creator.

Sephiroth simply shrugged.

"I am inclined to agree, Professor," Sephiroth said mildly. "And yet, here we are."
