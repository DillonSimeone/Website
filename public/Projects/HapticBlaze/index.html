1. Executive Summary

HapticBlaze is to tactile haptics what WLED is to addressable LEDs: a high-performance, open-source firmware that turns any ESP32 board and standard motor driver (L298N, DRV8833, DRV2605L, or discrete MOSFETs) into an instantly programmable, web-controlled haptic engine.

Historically, implementing high-fidelity haptic feedback has been highly fragmented. Developers were forced to hack low-level I2C registers by hand, write blocking delay loops in custom firmware, or build bespoke analog filter circuits. HapticBlaze solves this by introducing a unified **Hardware Abstraction Layer (HAL)**. One declarative pattern works flawlessly across LRAs, ERMs, solenoids, or high-amplitude voice coils without editing a single line of C++.

2. Hardware Abstraction Layer (HAL) Core Architecture

Traditional haptic programming requires hardware-specific codebases. An LRA requires AC frequency resonance tracking, while an ERM motor requires DC voltage thresholds. HapticBlaze solves this through a high-performance **HAL pipeline** implemented inside a FreeRTOS task running on CPU Core 1:

Dynamic Resonance Tracking

LRAs operate optimally only at their precise physical resonant frequency (typically 170–235 Hz). HapticBlaze continuously reads back-EMF voltage signals from the DRV2605L, adjusting the driver frequency dynamically in real-time.

Telemetry Waveform Synthesis

Declarative patterns are processed inside our flat-bytecode interpreter. The interpreter outputs normalized intensity maps [0.0–1.0], which are converted by the active HAL driver into precise voltage ramps or high-speed PWM cycles.

3. Acoustic Sensory Substitution

HapticBlaze features a high-performance 256-point Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) routing module that samples audio inputs (via an analog auxiliary port or I2S digital microphones). The output frequency spectrum is mapped directly into dynamic vibration envelopes. This represents a huge leap forward in open-source accessibility haptics, enabling the Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing (D/HoH) community to experience musical beats, environment cues, or alarm sirens in real-time.
