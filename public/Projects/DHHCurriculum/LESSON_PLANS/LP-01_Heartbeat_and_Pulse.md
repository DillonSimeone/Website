# LP-01 — Heartbeat & Pulse

**Grade band:** K-2 (FOUNDATION)
**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Class size:** Up to 8 students (paired with one PULSE vest each, or rotated in two cohorts)
**Prerequisites:** None.

## Learning Objectives
By the end of the lesson, each student will be able to:
1. Locate their own heartbeat and sign HEARTBEAT in ASL with appropriate non-manual marker.
2. Distinguish a fast pulse from a slow pulse felt on the PULSE vest.
3. Mirror a teacher-led tempo by tapping foot or hand.

## ASL Gloss Vocabulary (introduced)
| Sign | Gloss | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| HEARTBEAT | "feel your heart" | Closed fist tapping chest, sync to actual beat |
| FAST | "rapid pulse" | Standard sign; eyebrows up |
| SLOW | "calm pulse" | Standard sign; eyebrows neutral or down |
| LOUD | "big feel" | Sign LOUD with widened eyes |
| QUIET | "small feel" | Sign QUIET with pursed lips |
| FEEL | "tactile awareness" | Open hand sweeping over chest/arm |

## CyberDeck / PULSE Setup
- 1× CyberDeck (preset boot: `K2_Heartbeat.preset`)
- 1× PULSE vest per student (or shared across two-student rotation)
- Pre-loaded patch: a single low-frequency sine (60 Hz) gated to a tempo the teacher controls via a single physical dial on the CyberDeck front panel.
- Tempo range: 50-180 BPM.
- LED bar mirrors the pulse with one solid color (warm amber for slow, cool cyan for fast — linear hue interpolation handled by the patch).

**Pre-class teacher prep (5 min):**
1. Boot CyberDeck → select `K2_Heartbeat.preset`.
2. Verify all vests are charged (≥ 80%) and pair via BLE (LEDs on vest go solid blue on link).
3. Set initial tempo to a resting 72 BPM.

## Lesson Sequence
| Time | Activity | Teacher | Students |
|------|----------|---------|----------|
| 0:00-0:05 | Greeting in ASL; sign HEARTBEAT and have students locate their own pulse on neck/wrist. | Demonstrates; checks each student. | Find pulse, sign HEARTBEAT. |
| 0:05-0:12 | Vest fitting + first low-pulse stimulus at 60 BPM. | Walks through space; observes affect. | Wear vest; sign FEEL when they perceive pulse. |
| 0:12-0:20 | Tempo ladder: 60 → 90 → 120 → 60 BPM. At each step students sign FAST or SLOW. | Turns dial; freezes between steps. | Sign FAST/SLOW; tap foot to pulse. |
| 0:20-0:27 | Mirror game: teacher claps a pattern; one student wears vest receiving same pattern; other students mirror by clapping. | Models; rotates leader. | Mirror clap; rotate who wears vest. |
| 0:27-0:30 | Closing: sign HEARTBEAT, then sign QUIET, then turn off CyberDeck together. | Leads ritual close. | Sign with teacher. |

## Differentiation
- **K students:** Reduce to two contrasting tempos (60 vs. 120 BPM).
- **Hearing peers in inclusive classrooms:** Wear vest first; identify tempo without looking at LED. (See `CURRICULUM_MAP.md` mixed-hearing protocol.)
- **CI users:** Allow processors active or deactivated at student preference (per SA §3.1, NH model).
- **Sensory-sensitivity opt-out:** Vest worn loosely over a t-shirt; intensity dial reduced to 30%.

## Safety
Max vest intensity capped at 50% in the K-2 preset. Continuous vest-on time ≤ 15 min. If a student signs UNCOMFORTABLE or removes vest, no further encouragement to wear it; offer LED-only mode.

## Assessment Rubric

| Criterion | 4 — Exceeding | 3 — Meeting | 2 — Approaching | 1 — Emerging |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Locates pulse** | Locates own pulse + sibling's pulse and compares. | Locates own pulse independently. | Locates pulse with one prompt. | Cannot locate pulse after multiple prompts. |
| **Distinguishes fast/slow** | Identifies 3+ tempi correctly; ranks them. | Identifies fast vs. slow correctly across 3+ trials. | Identifies fast vs. slow in 1-2 trials. | No reliable distinction. |
| **ASL HEARTBEAT, FAST, SLOW** | Produces all three signs with NMMs. | Produces all three signs. | Produces 2 of 3. | Produces 0-1. |
| **Mirroring** | Leads a pattern other students mirror. | Mirrors teacher's pattern accurately. | Mirrors after 2nd modeling. | Cannot mirror after multiple models. |

**Evidence collected:** Teacher field notes (per NEA Data Management Plan p.18); short video clip of mirroring activity (with parental media-release on file); one photograph of completed-activity wall (student-drawn pulse pictures).
