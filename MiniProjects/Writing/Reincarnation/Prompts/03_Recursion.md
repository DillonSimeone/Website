# Prompt for Recursion

Marcus stared at what he'd made.

The party stared at him.

He became aware that he was, in the current situation, responsible for four human beings who were currently prisoners in a castle that would very much like to kill them.

Well, he thought. This is a problem I didn't scope.

He turned away from them and ran the variables.

Option one: kill them. Fast, clean, final. He thought about the cascade of eight becoming sixteen becoming a crusade and discarded this immediately.

Option two: hand them over to the Demon Lord. He thought about what would happen to them then and discarded this faster.

Option three: let them go where they were. They'd be found. Outcome identical to option two.

Option four: get them out.

He turned back to the party. They had not stopped staring at him.

"I'm going to put you to sleep," he said. His voice came out lower than he expected — Grak's register — but the words were his. "When you wake up, you'll be outside the castle. Don't come back."

The swordsman said something. It sounded like a question, or possibly a threat, or possibly both.

Marcus reached into the structure he'd built and adjusted a parameter.

They went to sleep.

He stood in the corridor for a moment with four unconscious heroes at his feet, in a demonic castle full of creatures who would happily kill all of them, and thought: I've made things significantly more complicated.

Then he started working on how to make things less complicated.

He carried them out one at a time in the deep hours, wrapped in a mana structure that made them invisible to casual observation. It took six hours. He put them in the field beyond the outer wall, arranged reasonably comfortably, with enough ambient warmth woven into the air around them that they wouldn't freeze before dawn.

He walked back to his post and resumed his duties.

Grak's background static said nothing, because Grak would not have known what to say.
