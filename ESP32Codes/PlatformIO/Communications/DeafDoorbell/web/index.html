Power & Assembly

Reliability starts with power. While standard USB cables work, we recommend modifying them for permanent installs.

Voltage Drop Note: A standard USB-A or USB-C cable can be modified into power lines roughly 30 to 50 feet long. While 5V is standard for USB, long runs can cause voltage drop. For runs over 20 feet, use thicker gauge wire to maintain the required 5V 1A output.

The Pro Way: Any USB power source will output 5V by default. For the cleanest setup, strip a USB-C cable and solder the wires directly to the 5V and GND pins on the ESP32-C3. This ensures maximum contact integrity and allows for stress-relieved wiring.

Stress Relief: Use Hot Glue generously for insulation and cable stress relief. We recommend gluing the ESP32 directly to the 3D-printed mounts for a rigid, vibration-proof assembly. The ESP32s are kept mostly out in the open for optimal cooling and visible status monitoring.
