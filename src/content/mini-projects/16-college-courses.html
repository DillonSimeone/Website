MathInArts - Experiments

A collection of mathematical visualizations developed for my Math in Arts course.

  • Unmasking: An interactive experiment in Sonic Agency. Using Mediapipe for real-time face and hand tracking, I implemented a Delaunay triangulation system that generates a playable mask.
    Launch Unmasking (Requires Camera)

  • Binary Visualizer: A 3D explorer for binary data. It maps trigrams (sequences of 3 bytes) to XYZ coordinates and uses entropy to determine brightness, revealing the "shape" of different file formats.
    Launch Binary Visualizer

  • Infinity Visualizer: A performance-optimized library of audio-reactive fractal and procedural shaders, exploring the beauty of Hausdorff dimensions.
    Launch Infinity Visualizer

CyclingMax - Audio Programming 1 & 2

An exploration into Max/MSP and visual programming. As a deaf developer, learning Max provided a unique way to architect sound through logic and flowcharts rather than traditional auditory feedback.

These projects were highly acclaimed by my professors for their bridge between raw data and musical experience:

  • Weather Sonification: A Node.js integration with the National Weather Service API that transforms real-time local temperature and wind speed into generative soundscapes.
  • Motion-Reactive Textures: Algorithms that analyze video feeds to generate musical accompaniment based on movement intensity.
  • Unmasking (Original Prototype): The first iteration of the Unmasking project, driving complex FM synthesis via real-time hand-tracking data.

Note: These projects require a Max/MSP environment to run.
