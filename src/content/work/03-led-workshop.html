LED Controller Workshop

I ran a workshop at CymaSpace’s Woodstock Cafe where participants built and programmed their own LED controllers using MicroBlocks flashed onto ESP32 SuperMinis.

The fun part? These specific ESP32 boards can be bought in bulk for under two dollars each. MicroBlocks makes them accessible to complete beginners while still being powerful enough for serious projects. Unlike Arduino C++, there’s no IDE to install, any device that can run Chrome can also program a MicroBlocks-compatible board, either by USB or over Bluetooth if the hardware supports it. That includes smartphones and tablets! (Horrible experience, but still...!)

I also 3D-printed the shells for the LED strips and demonstrated just how much physical abuse LED strips can take before failing, and how to repair them when they do. Nearly every part of the kits was 3D-printed, except for the LEDs and controllers.
