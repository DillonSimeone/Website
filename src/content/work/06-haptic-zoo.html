Haptic Zoo

Think petting zoo, but instead of goats and chickens, it’s a bunch of hacked-together machines that poke, buzz, rumble, and squish in strange ways. I set up a room full of different haptic devices so people could walk around and touch sound: bass thumps you can feel in your chest, fingertip buzzers that tickle like carbonated water, and low-frequency shakers that make tables feel alive. The goal was to explore how many ways we can send information through skin and bones instead of ears.

The event officially took place on August 30, 2024. You can view the original event listing at CymaSpace's Haptic Zoo Page. Note: The information in that original link is outdated/incorrect regarding what was actually shown.

What was actually deployed and experienced at the Haptic Zoo included:

  • GELU Bracers: Early prototypes of the GELU haptic bracers, providing direct tactile sensations to the wearer's arms.
  • Haptic Bench: A custom-built wooden haptic bench hooked up directly to an electric piano. When keys were pressed, the bench vibrated to let users feel the music's frequency and dynamics.
  • Percussion Pad & Bass Speaker: A percussion pad deployed alongside a large bass speaker for high-impact physical audio rumbles.
  • Wireless Haptic Vests: Woojer Haptic Vest 3s connected to a custom wireless RF transmitter system, allowing multiple vests to be controlled simultaneously from a second set of electric pianos.

Watching people bounce between devices, compare sensations, and then try to “play” them like instruments was the best part. I left with a pile of new ideas for haptic patterns, some hardware to rebuild, and a strong reminder that curiosity scales way better than volume knobs.
